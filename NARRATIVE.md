# Welcome to MoonDeck — Narrative & Presenter Notes

The story the deck tells, slide by slide: the one idea each slide carries, what's on
screen, what to say over it, and how it hands off. This deck is **meta** — the reader is
inside MoonDeck and will edit this very file. Keep the deck honest to this spine — if a
slide stops serving its beat, fix the slide, not the story.

## The thesis (one sentence)
You've already done the work — the ideas live in your code, your docs, your threads — so the
deck shouldn't cost you a night; an LLM speaks HTML natively, so let it build the slides while
you just talk.

## The arc
Open meta (you're reading this inside the product). Establish the real pain: the work is
already done, the night vanishes *re-assembling* it — and every tool so far was built for a
human to drive, so you're capped by one pair of hands. Turn on a 1999 insight: the browser is
Ronald Reagan embellishing a terse ticker tape, and an LLM speaks that ticker tape (HTML)
natively. So let the LLM do the work — and show how you actually use it: **connect** the model
you already pay for, then **tell it** what you want. Then show it running in the real app, hand
over the features, point at a text-based future (git), and give the user the keys — to this very deck.

## Slide-by-slide

### 1 — Welcome · *Cover*
**Idea:** You're reading this inside the product that built it.
**On screen:** "Welcome to MoonDeck" · "Describe your idea. Watch it come to life."
**Say:** "Everything you're about to see, MoonDeck built. You're looking at it right now — and by the end you'll edit this very deck."
**Hands off:** before the *how*, here's the pain it removes →

### 2 — You already did the work · *Problem*
**Idea:** The ideas already exist; what eats the night is re-assembling them into slides.
**On screen:** four source cards — your codebase · dashboards & metrics · Slack threads · PRDs & BRDs → arrow → "a deck"; punch: the thinking's done, the assembly is the tax.
**Say:** "You already did the work. It's in your codebase, your dashboards, a Slack thread, a PRD. The ideas exist. What vanishes the night is re-typing them into boxes."
**Hands off:** and that re-assembly isn't cheap →

### 3 — 100 hours a year · *Problem (the cost)*
**Idea:** The assembly tax is enormous — and MoonDeck collapses it.
**On screen:** bar chart — one deck by hand **8 hrs** → with MoonDeck **5 min**; punch "turn your ideas into visuals in minutes, not hours"; source line (INNOFACT, ~40% is formatting).
**Say:** "A real deck is most of a workday. Across a year that's ~100 hours — two and a half work weeks — and ~40% of it is pure formatting. MoonDeck does the same deck in minutes."
**Hands off:** so why has it stayed this expensive? →

### 4 — Built for humans · *Problem*
**Idea:** Every tool was built for a human — and this one isn't. (Headline: "Every tool was built for a human.")
**On screen:** three cards — Built for humans (PowerPoint · Slides · Figma) · Built for developers (Slidev · Reveal) · Built for agents (MoonDeck, highlighted). No subtitle — the headline + cards carry it.
**Say:** "PowerPoint, Slides, Figma — built for humans to operate. Slidev, Reveal — built for developers, who are still humans. MoonDeck is the first built for the agent to drive."
**Hands off:** there's a 1999 idea that flips this →

### 5 — The quote · *Insight*
**Idea:** The browser is a vivid narrator reading a terse script — Stephenson saw it in 1999.
**On screen:** just the pull quote + attribution — "The HTML files are the pithy description on the paper tape, and your Web browser is Ronald Reagan." — Neal Stephenson, *In the Beginning… Was the Command Line* (1999). No context line; the payoff lands on the next slide.
**Say:** "Reagan, before he was president, called baseball games he couldn't see — reading a terse ticker tape and embellishing every pitch into a vivid broadcast. Stephenson said the web works the same way: HTML is the ticker tape, the browser is Reagan."
**Hands off:** so who writes the ticker tape now? →

### 6 — What MoonDeck is · *What we do*
**Idea:** MoonDeck brings your ideas to life — the payoff of the quote.
**On screen:** "MoonDeck brings your ideas to life." · one supporting line — an LLM speaks HTML natively, so it writes your deck while you just talk.
**Say:** "A slide is just HTML, and HTML is the one thing an LLM produces effortlessly. The problem was never PowerPoint skill — so just say the idea, and MoonDeck builds it."
**Hands off:** using it is two steps — first, connect your model →

### 7 — Bring your own LLM · *How you use it (step 1)*
**Idea:** Step 1 — connect the LLM you already use; no new seat.
**On screen:** feature split — "Works with the LLMs you love" + one line + the Claude/ChatGPT marks, beside the animated sign-in screen.

### 8 — Tell it what to do · *How you use it (step 2) — the heart*
**Idea:** Step 2 — say what you want in plain words, and it builds it.
**On screen:** before → ask → after — a blank slide → your instruction ("Add our Q3 traction — we grew from 8M to 32M users") → the built "Traction · 4× in a year · From 8M to 32M users" slide with the logo.
**Say:** "Step two is the whole product: you just say what you want. 'Add our Q3 traction, we grew from 8 to 32 million.' It writes the slide — number, chart, logo — while you watch."
**Hands off:** and this isn't a mockup — it's the app you're in →

### 12 — A deck is just text · *Roadmap*
**Idea:** Because a deck is just text, nothing is ever lost and sharing is trivial — your work is auto-saved and restorable today, and one word turns it into a website; building together is next.
**On screen:** three cards — auto-saved (live), publish it (live), collaborate (next).
**Say:** "A MoonDeck deck is just text under the hood, so it behaves like nothing you've used before. Every change is saved automatically — you can jump back to any earlier version. One word turns it into a live website you can share. And soon, you and your team build the same deck together." (Keep it about what they get — never mention git or GitHub.)
**Hands off:** so — your turn →

**Idea:** Make *this* deck yours, right now.
**On screen:** "Now make it yours." · connect Claude or ChatGPT, then tell your agent what to change — even the whole vibe.
**Say:** "Everything you saw, you can do to this deck right now. Connect your LLM and just tell your agent: change this slide — or make me a deck about anything."
**The action:** connect an LLM and edit this deck.
